Step by Step

The MP Drafting Process

1

Submit Your Project

Use the quote form to tell us about your project. Upload your architectural drawings, select a level of detail, and provide basic project information. No account required.

  • Select level of detail: Quick Draw, Typical Detail, or Advanced Detailing
  • Describe the project scope and system type
  • Upload PDF or DWG architectural drawings
  • Indicate whether engineering coordination is needed
The quote form is designed to capture what we need upfront — so the first conversation is about scope and timeline, not "can you send more info?"
Start the Quote Form →
2

Kickoff & Scoping

We review your documents and return a clear scope confirmation — level of detail, system types, page count estimate, timeline, and price. No vague estimates.

  • Document review: architectural drawings, existing submittals, specs
  • Scope confirmation with line-item breakdown
  • Timeline commitment with key milestones
  • Any clarification questions resolved before production begins
If there are questions about scope, we ask them here — before the clock starts. You approve the scope before we begin.
3

Production

Drawing production begins. We work from your architectural set, coordinating actual field conditions — not generic templates. Fasteners are matched to conditions. Surrounding conditions are drawn accurately. Column lines are referenced.

  • Conditions pulled from architectural drawings, not assumed
  • Fasteners specified and matched to condition type
  • IN/OUT dimensions calculated and verified
  • Column lines and layout referenced throughout
  • Clarifying notes drafted for estimated or missing information
For Advanced Detailing projects, production is a live collaboration. Drawings evolve with the project. We coordinate with your architect and engineer in real time.
4

Preview Stage

Before we finalize, you review a working set. This is the MP process difference — the preview stage eliminates surprise revisions and ensures alignment before final production.

  • Preview set delivered at agreed milestone
  • You review and provide consolidated feedback
  • Changes addressed before final production
  • No surprise revisions after final delivery
The preview stage is where most revision cycles end. You see the work, provide feedback, and final delivery reflects it — clean and complete.
5

Final Delivery

Finished drawings delivered in your required format — PDF, DWG, or both. Architect-ready and organized for submittal. No babysitting required.

  • Fully coordinated, submittal-ready drawing set
  • PDF and/or DWG delivery
  • Organized with title blocks, sheet numbering, and revision tracking
  • Archived for future reference and revision requests
After delivery, your drawings are archived. If you need a revision, an update, or a reference to a previous project — we have it.

Engineering is available as a bundled service through our extended network. When needed, we align the drafting scope with the engineering scope so both deliver together.
