Quality & Scope

We specialize exclusively in commercial glazing — curtain wall, storefront, all-glass entrances, railings, metal panel, and related systems. Our drawings reflect actual field conditions, not manufacturer templates. That means accurate surrounding conditions drawn from your architectural set, fasteners matched to condition type, IN/OUT dimensions, column lines, and submittals that architects actually approve the first time.
Engineering validates structural performance. Shop drawings coordinate reality. Our drawings integrate architectural inconsistencies, resolve fabrication-level detail, clarify fastener selection, and give installers the layout clarity they need. Both roles are necessary — and when they're aligned from the start, projects run smoother and RFIs go down.
This is a common assumption that often creates more work downstream. Engineers validate structural performance — they don't coordinate surrounding conditions, specify fasteners to condition, or integrate the architectural inconsistencies that make shop drawings complex. When drafting starts from the engineering rather than from the architecture, you often end up with drawings that pass the structural check but still need significant coordination before they're usable on the job.
Quick Draw is right for early-stage projects, approval-level submittals, or jobs where budget is a constraint and full coordination isn't required yet. Typical Detail is the MP standard — right for most commercial submittals where accuracy and architect-ready presentation matter. Design Build is for projects where you're in the active design phase and need live collaboration with the architect and engineer. When in doubt, the quote form walks you through it and we'll recommend a level after reviewing your drawings.

Process & Timeline

Timeline depends on project scope, system complexity, and current production load. We provide a clear timeline commitment with your quote — not a vague estimate. Turnaround is part of our scope confirmation and we don't start production until that timeline is agreed.
The preview stage is a working set delivered mid-production for your review before we finalize. It's the step that eliminates surprise revisions at the end. You review, provide consolidated feedback, and we address changes before final delivery. Most revision cycles end at the preview stage — not after final delivery.
Reach out directly through the contact page. We'll assess current production capacity and be transparent about what's realistic. We don't take on rush work and overpromise — if we can hit your timeline, we'll tell you. If we can't, we'll tell you that too.

Files & Formats

We accept PDF and DWG architectural drawings. Upload directly through the quote form. If you have other formats (RVT, DXF, etc.), reach out first and we'll confirm compatibility.
Final drawings are delivered as PDF and/or DWG depending on your requirements. Deliverables include a complete, organized drawing set with title blocks, sheet numbering, and revision tracking. All project files are archived for future reference.
Yes. BIM and 3D services are available as part of our service scope. This includes Revit modeling for glazing systems, coordination with project BIM models, and LOD-compliant deliverables. Specify this requirement when submitting your quote.
